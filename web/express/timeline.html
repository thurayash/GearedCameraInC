Start Line

This was our beginning. Our group had formally regestired, and we laucnhed the project! With haste, we divided our work to compliment each other's strengths. Yassine had to work on the hardware, Thuraya and Maxence had to work on Image Re-coloration, and Karim had to Harvest Data from said re-colored images.

Get The Ball Rolling

With momentum and vigor, we persevered. We had finished getting sufficient data from our images, and we had finished the blueprints for our hardware. We just had to 3D print components, and then procur the electronics. Additionally, we had started outlining the facial recognition algorithms in our program (Without Pesky AI !).

Hard Work

OOOOOOOOHH WOOOOOAAAH! WE'RE HALF WAY THERE! From here on out are critical times. Crunch culture at its finest dare we say. The first prototype was printed, assembled, and constructed. We had something working! Buzzing with lights even! And the cherry on top was our own brand of face detection algorithms working suffciently well.

Upgrades, People. Upgrades.

Here we are, with multiple optimizations being carried out to make our Project run seemlessly and flawlessly. For starters, we revisited our prototype and decided to make sturdier, more capable in terms of strength and torque. Secondly, we went above and beyond to hone our face detection algos. Finally, we started to carry out something of a nightmarish realm! TRIGONOMETRY! (Karim and Yassine would like to punch Pythagoras for inventing his blasted theorem).

Time To Party

GAME! SET! We finished our project well before the final deadline, leaving room for many more optimizations and revists. At last, we have a working product that truly makes one wonder ": just how much effort was poured into this?". With humble pride, we present to you GCC, the camera that Mark Zuckerberg will definitely use to spy on you.(non)
